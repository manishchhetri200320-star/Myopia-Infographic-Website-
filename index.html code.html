What is Myopia?

Myopia, also called nearsightedness, is a common condition where distant objects appear blurry while close objects can be seen clearly. It occurs when the eye grows too long, causing light to focus in front of the retina instead of on it.

Estimated 5 billion people will be myopic by 2050! There is no "safe" level of myopia, so the higher the script, the higher risks of complications mentioned below.

Myopia typically develops in childhood and can progress over time. Understanding it early helps in choosing effective strategies to slow its progression.

Why Myopia Matters

Progressive myopia increases the risk of certain eye conditions. These visuals help explain what each one means:

Retinal Detachment

Stretching of the retina in longer eyes can lead to thinning, tears, or detachment. Sudden floaters, flashes, or a curtain over vision need urgent care.

Myopic Maculopathy

Damage to the macula (central vision) can reduce detailed vision. Regular eye checks help detect early changes.

Open-Angle Glaucoma

Myopia increases the chance of optic nerve damage over time. Pressure checks and imaging guide monitoring.

Early Cataracts

Lens clouding can appear earlier in myopic individuals. Symptoms include blur, glare at night, and reduced contrast.

Higher Risk of Retinal Degenerations

Conditions such as lattice degeneration and pavingstone changes occur more often in myopia, increasing long-term risk for complications.

Lifestyle Impact

Uncorrected myopia can affect academics, risk amblyopia or strabismus, impact self-image, and add costs and inconvenience to daily life.

Treatment Options

Several strategies exist to slow myopia progression and maintain healthy vision:

Glasses

Specially designed myopia control lenses are proven to slow progression such as Hoya MiyoSmart and Essilor Stellest.

Contact Lenses

Multifocal and orthokeratology lenses help correct vision and manage eye growth.

Low-Dose Atropine Drops

Safe, low-dose eye drops can slow the progression of myopia in children under professional supervision.

Outdoor Time

Regular outdoor activity has been shown to reduce onset of myopia in children.

Vision Hygiene

Limiting near-work time and ensuring breaks reduces eye strain.

Combination Approaches

Using glasses, contacts, outdoor time, and drops together can provide stronger protection against myopia progression.

Multifocal Lenses

Multifocal contact lenses provide clear vision at multiple distances and are effective in controlling myopia progression:

How they work

Multifocal lenses have different zones for seeing at various distances (near, intermediate and distance). The central zone provides clear distance vision, while the outer zones reduce the signal that causes the eye to elongate, therefore slowing the progression of myopia.

Clinical findings

Study conducted by (Rafa et al., 2021) showed that Myopia progression was controlled by 38.6% and 66.6% in children wearing Multistage + 1.50D and Proclear +3.00D MFCL, respectively, in comparison to children wearing SVCL over an 18-month period.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are multifocal contact lenses safe for children? +
Yes, with proper hygiene and supervision, multifocal lenses are safe for children. The risk of infection is very low when lenses are cared for correctly.
At what age can my child start wearing contact lenses? +
Most children can start around 8‚Äì12 years old, depending on their maturity and ability to handle lens care responsibly. Adults can also use multifocal contact lenses but they are more common in aiding with presbyopia management rather than myopia.
Do multifocal lenses cure myopia? +
No, they don‚Äôt cure myopia, but they slow its progression and provide clear vision at different distances.
What is the difference between daily and monthly contact lenses? +
Dialy lenses are worn once and discarded at the end of the day, offering convenience and lower infection risk. Monthly lenses are reused for up to 30 days but it does require proper cleaning and storing habits. Both treatment options have been shown to reduce myopia progression by 30-50%. Daily lenses may be more effective while monthly lenses are cheaper.
How to use multifocal contact lenses safely? +
Wash your hands thoroughly with soap, rinse and dry with a clean lint-free towel before applying the lenses. Monthly lenses should be cleaned daily with a multipurpose solution and stored in a specialised case with contact lens disinfection solution. Follow your eyecare professional's advice and instructions.
